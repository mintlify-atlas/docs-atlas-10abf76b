---
title: "Sharing palettes"
description: "Copy a link that encodes your palette colors directly in the URL. Anyone who opens the link sees the exact same palette, with lock states preserved."
---

Press `C` to copy a share link to your clipboard. The link encodes your current palette colors (and optionally their lock states) as URL parameters, so no account or server is needed — the entire palette lives in the URL.

## URL format

Share links use the `?colors` query parameter. Each color is represented as a 6-character hex value **without** the `#` prefix, separated by hyphens:

```
https://example.com/?colors=ff5733-3498db-2ecc71
```

If any colors are locked, a `?locked` parameter is added alongside `?colors`. Each position maps to `1` (locked) or `0` (unlocked):

```
https://example.com/?colors=ff5733-3498db-2ecc71&locked=1-0-1
```

<Note>
  The `?locked` parameter is only included when at least one color is locked. If no colors are locked, the parameter is omitted entirely.
</Note>

## Loading a shared palette

When you visit a URL that contains a `?colors` parameter, the app automatically loads the encoded palette on startup:

1. The colors in the URL replace the current palette.
2. Lock states are restored from the `?locked` parameter, if present. If the `?locked` parameter is absent, **all loaded colors are locked by default** so you can reroll the unlocked positions freely.
3. The URL parameters are cleared from the browser's address bar (`history.replaceState`) after loading, so sharing or bookmarking the page afterwards does not re-encode the palette.

<Tip>
  Because all colors default to locked when loading from a URL, you can immediately press `R` to reroll and generate new palettes that keep the shared colors as anchors.
</Tip>

## Lock states in share links

When you press `C`, the current lock state of every color is included in the link. This means the recipient sees the same locked/unlocked configuration you had when you copied the link.

Lock states are encoded as a `-`-separated string of `1`s and `0`s in positional order. The app pads or trims the `?locked` value to match the number of colors in `?colors` if they ever differ.

## Practical uses

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Share with a team">
    Generate a palette you like, press `C`, and paste the link in a chat or email. Your team opens the exact same palette in their browser with no sign-up required.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Save a specific palette state">
    Bookmark the share link to save a palette outside of the browser's local storage. This is useful when switching between devices or sharing across browsers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Embed in messages or documents">
    Paste the link directly into a Notion page, Slack message, Jira ticket, or any tool that renders URLs. Anyone who clicks it opens the palette instantly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Anchor colors for a team reroll">
    Share a link with specific colors locked. Recipients load the palette, unlock the colors they want to vary, and reroll to explore variations around your shared anchors.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Limitations

- The URL encodes hex values only — color names, tags, and collection metadata are not included.
- Very long palettes (many colors) can produce long URLs, though in practice palettes are small enough that this is not an issue.
- The share link reflects the palette at the moment you pressed `C`. If you make changes afterwards, press `C` again to get a fresh link.
